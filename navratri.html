About Navratri

Navratri is a Hindu festival celebrated over nine nights and ten days. It honors the goddess Durga and her various forms. The word "Navratri" is derived from two Sanskrit words: "Nav" meaning nine and "Ratri" meaning night. During this festival, people observe fasting, perform religious rituals, and participate in vibrant dance forms like Garba and Dandiya. Each of the nine nights is dedicated to a different form of goddess Durga, and devotees seek her blessings for prosperity and protection. Navratri is celebrated with great enthusiasm and devotion in various parts of India, with colorful decorations, traditional attire, and music adding to the festive atmosphere. It typically occurs in the months of September or October.

Dashera

Dussehra, also known as Vijayadashami, is a significant Hindu festival celebrated in India and other parts of the world. It typically falls in the month of September or October, marking the triumph of good over evil. The festival spans ten days and concludes on the day of Dussehra. The central theme of Dussehra is the legendary battle between Lord Rama and the demon king Ravana. It symbolizes the victory of righteousness (Rama) over wickedness (Ravana) and the restoration of dharma (righteousness). The most prominent tradition during Dussehra is the reenactment of this battle through Ramlila, a dramatic representation of Lord Rama's life story. On the tenth day of Dussehra, effigies of Ravana, his brother Kumbhakarna, and his son Meghnad are burnt in large public gatherings, signifying the destruction of evil forces. This event, known as Ravana Dahan, draws huge crowds.
