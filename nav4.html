DAY-4 CELEBRATION

On the fourth day of Navratri, Hindus across India and around the world celebrate with great enthusiasm and devotion. This day is dedicated to the worship of Goddess Kushmanda, the fourth manifestation of Goddess Durga. Devotees wake up early, take a purifying bath, and offer prayers to the deity with utmost devotion. The color of the day is associated with red, symbolizing vigor and energy, and many devotees choose to wear red attire or accessories. The evening festivities come alive with mesmerizing dandiya and garba dances, where people gather in colorful traditional attire to dance in circles, holding wooden sticks or clapping their hands to the rhythmic beats of the music. These dance forms symbolize the triumph of good over evil and create a sense of unity and joy within the community. Temples are adorned with flowers and lights, and devotees participate in cultural programs and musical performances. It's a day filled with spirituality, music, dance, and a strong sense of togetherness, marking the continued celebration of Navratri's nine divine nights.
